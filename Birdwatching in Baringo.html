Explore more than 400 Bird's Species!!

Lake Baringo, nestled within Kenya's Great Rift Valley, stands as a premier birdwatching destination, attracting enthusiasts from around the globe. Its diverse habitats, ranging from the lake itself to surrounding scrublands and cliffs, support an astonishing variety of avian life. Here's a deeper exploration of birdwatching in this remarkable location:

A Birdwatcher's Paradise:

Avian Diversity:

Lake Baringo boasts an impressive checklist of over 470 bird species, making it a true haven for bird lovers. This sheer number of species ensures that every visit offers the potential for new and exciting sightings.

The lake's popularity among birders is also due to the ease of viewing many species. It is possible to see a large number of species in a relatively short period of time.

Habitat Variety:

The lake's open waters, papyrus swamps, acacia woodlands, and rocky cliffs create a mosaic of habitats, each supporting its own unique bird communities.

This diversity is crucial for attracting such a wide range of bird species, from waterbirds to raptors and everything in between.

Key Bird Groups:

Wading Birds:

Lake Baringo is a prime location for observing various wading birds include: Herons (Goliath Heron, Grey Heron, etc.), Egrets, Storks (African Openbill, Marabou Stork, etc.), Various plovers and sandpipers.

Nocturnal Birds:

The lake's surroundings also provide habitat for a variety of nocturnal birds. Owls are particularly well-represented, with species like the: Spotted Eagle-Owl, Verreaux's Eagle-Owl, Cape Eagle Owl, White faced scops owl, Standard-winged Nightjars can also be spotted.

• Migratory Birds: o Lake Baringo serves as an important stopover for migratory birds, particularly during the northern hemisphere's winter months. o These seasonal visitors add to the already impressive bird diversity, with species arriving from Europe and Asia. o Many different types of waders migrate through the area. • Indigenous Birds: o The lake and its surrounding areas are home to numerous indigenous bird species, many of which are unique to the region. o Some of the notable indigenous birds include:  Hemprich's Hornbill  Jackson's Hornbill  Bristle-crowned Starling  Various species of weavers.  Lichtenstein's Sandgrouse. Birdwatching Tips and Locations: • Best Times to Visit: o Early mornings and late afternoons are generally the most productive times for birdwatching, as birds are most active during these periods. o The dry season is often preferred, as water levels are lower, making it easier to observe waterbirds. • Key Birding Spots: o Lake Shores: The lake's shores provide excellent opportunities for observing wading birds and waterbirds. o Cliffs: The cliffs surrounding the lake are home to raptors and other cliff-dwelling birds. o Acacia Woodlands: The surrounding woodlands are ideal for observing a variety of songbirds and other terrestrial species. o Boat Trips: Taking a boat trip on the lake offers a unique perspective and allows access to areas that are otherwise difficult to reach. o Popular locations include areas around Roberts camp, and the Lake Baringo Club. • Essential Equipment: o Binoculars are essential for birdwatching. o A field guide to East African birds is highly recommended. o A telescope can be useful for observing distant birds. o Sunscreen and a hat are important for protection from the sun. Conservation: • It's important to practice responsible birdwatching, minimizing disturbance to birds and their habitats. • Supporting local conservation efforts helps to protect the lake's valuable birdlife. Lake Baringo offers an unparalleled birdwatching experience, with its stunning scenery and abundant avian diversity. Whether you're a seasoned birder or a novice enthusiast, a visit to this remarkable location is sure to be a memorable experience.

Boat riding in lake Baringo is a must to do activity, whether you are a birder, traveller, honeymooner or an adventurist happen to visit or planning to visit lake Baringo. There is so much to explore while boating in the lake. Be it along the banks, to the Islands, to the giraffe conservancy or to the communities living around the lake.

There are two types of boat riding offered in lake Baringo by Baringo Tours, Boats and Excursions, the morning sunrise boat riding and the sun downer boat riding in the evening. The best time to start a boat riding is as early as 5:30 am or 6:00 am. While on a boat, you can vividly the great rift valley formation. On the west, the progressive formation process that resulted to formation of Tugen hills in a stair case manner. On the east, you will see Laikipia escarpment.

Lake Baringo has seven Islands, each with unique stories and myths. Lake Baringo Hotsprings is found in Ol-kokwe Island. The community living on the Island are the Ilchamus community. On the Island, there are two primary schools and a dispensary. You can enjoy a boat to the hot springs and the community village visits. Other activities on the Island include, egg boiling and natural steam bathing.

Lake Baringo being an Important bird area (IBA) and is referred to as the birder’s paradise for hosting more that 470 species of Birds. A significant number of birds are found along the shores and in the Islands. Starting from herons, Egrets, kingfishers, pelicans, Comorans, fish eagle, bee eaters, hamerkops etc all these and many other species can be seen only by boat riding while in lake Baringo.

Other birds species found on the mainland includes: African paradise flycatcher, violet wood hoopoe, Abyssinian scimitar bill, African wattled lapwing, Black-headed lapwing, Black-necked grebe, Black-tailed godwit, Cape teal, Darnauds barbet, Egyptian vulture, Golden-backed weaver, Greater painted-snipe, Grosbeak canary, Lesser Honeyguide, Narina trogon, Rufous chatterer, Rufous-crowned roller, Sombre nightjar, Southern Pochard, Steel-blue whydah, Temmincks stint, Verreauxs eagle, White-billed buffalo-weaver and many more

Talking about the endangered Rothschild giraffe species which are in Ruko community conservancy. To access the conservancy by two ways. By road, or by boat. Using a boat is the safest and most explorable way because along the shore you will see crocodiles, hippos, birds, fishermen, fishing methods used in the lake, community villages, hot springs and fish eagle feeding at the Samatian Island. While in the Conservancy, you will enjoy giraffe walk and nature inside the conservancy. The giraffe population is now 18 in total and family of 2. You will also be able to Ostriches, Kudu, Warthogs and Waterbucks can be spotted very early in the morning.
